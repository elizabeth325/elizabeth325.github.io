Germany's Gawain

Germany may be geographically far from the Welsh landscapes where Gawain's adventures take place, but it is where some of his greatest stories were written.

Parzifal
The most famous German Arthurian story is Parzifal by Wolfram von Eschenbach. Based on the earlier Peredur and starring the same protaganist, Parzifal adapted it to a more complex writing style and established it as one of the iconic Arthurian moments and thereby one of Gawain's cornerstones.
Diu Krone
Diu Krone is another account of the quest for the Holy Grail. However, it is unique in that, while most grail quests star a pious hero without much history at court, Diu Krone is the only known text where Gawain finds the Holy Grail.
