The first Gawain stories to be told outside of Wales were written in France by a man named Chretien de Troyes. His works remain some of the most beloved in the entirety of Arthurian legend.

Yvain
Yvain by Chretien de Troyes is heralded as one of the greatest texts written in medieval Europe. Like many Arthurian texts it features a protagonist who goes to Gawain for help and ends up ceeding the role of main character to him.
The Knight of the Cart
The Knight of the Cart is significant in Gawain's timeline for introducing his best friend and greatest rival: Sir Lancelot.
