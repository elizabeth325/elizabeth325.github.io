English folksong "Sir Gawain" by Derek Fiechter and Brandon Fiechter
Sir Gawain and the Green Knight
The most famous characterization of Gawain appears in the Middle-English poem Sir Gawain and the Green Knight. This Gawain was created to exemplify the old knightly ideals in a world which was starting to value courtly love over true honor.
The Wedding of Sir Gawain and Dame Ragnelle
The Wedding of Sir Gawain and Dame Ragnelle is an example of the classic Loathy Lady tale. Modern readers understand it as a reversed Beauty and the Beast.
Le Morte d'Arthur
The plotlines of Le Morte d'Arthur are familiar to many readers because almost all modern adaptations are based on Le Morte d'Arthur. It covers Arthur's life from his birth to his death which it is named for.
